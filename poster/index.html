SHACL and ShEx in the Wild

A Community Survey on Validating Shapes Generation and Adoption

Kashif Rabbani, Matteo Lissandrini, and Katja Hose
Aalborg University, Denmark

There is a connection between the limitations of existing shapes extraction approaches in effectively supporting the real user needs and the common practice of generating shapes manually.

Our findings revealed that existing shapes extraction tools are capable of handling only relatively small knowledge graphs, are unable to extract all the required shapes constraints, and are prone to output spurious or erroneous shapes.

More research is needed to help users generate useful validating shapes for existing large knowledge graphs.

53% of respondents were from Industry, 27% from Academia and 20% from both (Fig a). Similarly, 38% of the respondents use graphs of size containing 1-100K triples (Fig b), 48% of respondents use graphs containing more than 10 - 100 classes in the ontology (Fig c), 50% of respondents use graphs containing more than 10-50 distinct properties (Fig d), 47% of respondents generate more than 10-100 shapes (Fig e), and 55% of respondents generate shapes for the entire graph whereas 45% generate shapes for some portions of the graph (Fig f).
